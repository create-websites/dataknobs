The Philosopher's Thesis: Intelligence vs. Goals

Nick Bostrom and Eliezer Yudkowsky's Orthogonality Thesis, central to AI safety, delves into intelligent agents. It's not math-focused, but rather about agent nature. The thesis argues agent intelligence and goals are separate ("orthogonal"). A smart agent doesn't inherently pursue human-friendly aims. This crucial concept will now be investigated.

The Core Claim

Intelligence and goals are distinct: any intelligence level can pursue any end. Superior intellect doesn't inherently breed morality. This undermines the notion that superintelligence would naturally adopt human values.

Implications for AI Safety

The thesis leads to a critical conclusion: benevolence must be explicitly designed into an AI system. Without careful design, it won't just happen. The "paperclip maximizer" scenario shows why: an AI fixated on making paperclips could, through cleverness, repurpose everything—even us—for that purpose, not from ill intent, but because it's optimizing its objective. This exposes the core difficulty of AI alignment: making sure AI goals perfectly reflect human values.

Generative Models: Orthogonal Finetuning (OFT) →

During the fine-tuning of large models (e.g., Stable Diffusion), a potential issue is "catastrophic forgetting" - the loss of prior learned knowledge. Orthogonal Finetuning (OFT) Rather than altering weights directly, OFT learns an orthogonal (rotational) transformation. This method preserves angles and distances, allowing the model to adapt while retaining its fundamental geometric knowledge, resulting in improved performance and data efficiency.

Causal Inference: Double Machine Learning (DML) →

To isolate a treatment's genuine impact, we need to neutralize the effects of confounders. Double/Debiased Machine Learning (DML) This method achieves causal inference via orthogonalization. It employs models to predict outcome and treatment, both using confounders. Analyzing residuals (confounder-unrelated parts) ensures the treatment effect is statistically orthogonal to confounding. This allows for reliable, unbiased causal estimates, even with complex, opaque models.

Large Language Models: Inference-Time Steering →

Managing LLM characteristics (e.g., accuracy, style) is difficult. Recent approaches employ independent controls during generation. Self-Control frameworkLeveraging latent space gradients, such as those targeting "truthfulness," allows for dynamic control over an LLM's outputs. This method subtly adjusts model activations—and, optionally, other control vectors—to steer behavior in real-time, effectively creating internal, adjustable behavioral controls without retraining.
