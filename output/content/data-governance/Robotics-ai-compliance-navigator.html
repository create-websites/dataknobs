The Compliance Process Flow

Achieving compliance is a systematic journey. This interactive diagram outlines the typical lifecycle for bringing a compliant robotics system to market. Click each step to learn more.

1. Design & Development

This foundational phase involves integrating relevant standards (like ISO 10218) directly into the robot's mechanical, electrical, and software architecture. Safety is designed-in, not bolted-on.

→
2. Risk Assessment

A systematic process (guided by standards like ISO 14971 for medical devices) to identify, analyze, and evaluate potential hazards throughout the robot's lifecycle, from normal operation to foreseeable misuse.

→
3. Testing & Validation

Rigorous testing of hardware and software to verify that safety functions and mitigations identified in the risk assessment perform as intended under both normal and fault conditions.

→
4. Certification

An independent audit by a notified body or accredited lab (e.g., TÜV, UL) that confirms the robot and its documentation meet all requirements of the relevant standards, resulting in a certification mark (e.g., CE, UL).
