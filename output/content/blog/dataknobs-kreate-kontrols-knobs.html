KONTROLS: A Holistic Governance Framework

KONTROLS provides a multi-layered approach to AI governance, ensuring security, compliance, and ethical deployment across the entire product lifecycle.

Multi-Layered Protection

Our framework moves beyond a simple checklist to provide comprehensive oversight. From the data itself to the infrastructure it runs on, every layer is secured and monitored. Hover over the chart to see the different control dimensions.

  • ✓Data & Model Centric: Ensures data quality, lineage, bias detection, and versioning.
  • ✓Output & Infrastructure Centric: Validates AI outputs and secures the underlying environment.
  • ✓Governance & Business Centric: Manages policies, audit trails, and aligns with business ROI.

KNOBS: Diagnose and Experiment

The optimization layer that democratizes system tuning, allowing users to perfect data products through systematic testing and virtual simulation.

A/B Experimentation Platform

Seamlessly integrated for versatile testing. Optimize websites, compare LLMs, test vector databases, and fine-tune retrieval strategies to find what works best. Predictive models can even suggest variations likely to succeed.

AI Twin for Virtual Optimization

A digital mirror of your physical IoT assets. Use virtual "knobs" to test production tweaks in a safe environment before real-world deployment. This mitigates risk and accelerates innovation in predictive maintenance and asset optimization.

Synergy in Action: The "Drive Train" Approach

KREATE, KONTROLS, and KNOBS work in a continuous feedback loop, powering a systematic process for building data products that deliver actionable results.

1

Define Goal & Controllable Levers

Clearly articulate the desired outcome and identify the "knobs" or parameters that can be manipulated to influence it.

2

Collect Data & Build Model

Gather necessary data and develop a model that reflects how the levers affect the outcome.

3

Process, Deploy & Monitor

Clean and process data, deploy the model into production, and continuously monitor its performance and accuracy.

4

Optimize with "What If" Scenarios

Use simulators to ask "What If" questions, feeding results into an optimizer to find the best solutions and avoid negative outcomes.
